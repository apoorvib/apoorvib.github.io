Who I Am

I'm a double major in Mathematics and Computer Science at New York University, passionate about applying mathematical principles to solve real-world problems in finance and data science. My research spans from creating novel quantitative financial models to optimizing urban infrastructure.

Currently, I'm focused on developing the Tail-Risk Adjusted Correlation Portfolio (TRACP) framework at NYU's Courant Institute, which tackles the critical "correlation breakdown" problem in financial markets during crashes. I'm also expanding my research into asymmetric correlation measures and pairs trading frameworks.

Quantitative Finance Research Assistant (TRACP)
