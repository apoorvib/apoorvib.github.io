Research Experience

Tail-Risk Adjusted Correlation Portfolio (TRACP) Framework

NYU Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences February 2025 - Present

Designed an innovative framework that solves the critical "correlation breakdown" problem where diversification fails during market crashes. My approach identifies "true diversifiers" assets that maintain their protective qualities precisely when investors need them most.

Currently developing a bimodal correlation model with regime-specific optimization that balances normal-market performance with enhanced protection during extreme events.

Advanced Pairs Trading Framework

NYU Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences February 2025 - Present

Developing a sophisticated pairs trading framework that captures lead-lag relationships between assets. Creating asymmetric and conditional correlation measures that dynamically adjust to market conditions.

Establishing a mathematical foundation for identifying and exploiting cross-asset pricing inefficiencies without relying on problematic multi-regime switching models.

Applied Monte Carlo methods to improve real-time decision-making in Monopoly (real-estate board game). Built computer simulations to test and validate adaptive strategies, demonstrating how probabilistic, data-driven shifts can lead to better outcomes.

EV Charging Infrastructure Optimization

NYU Center for Urban Science and Progress (CUSP) May 2024 - Present

Evaluated four charging infrastructure scenarios to optimize deployment while maintaining operational feasibility for NYC's 30,000+ vehicle municipal fleet. Implemented spatial hotspot analysis using Local Moran's I statistics to identify statistically significant charging demand patterns across Brooklyn neighborhoods.

Demonstrated that shared charging infrastructure between municipal EV fleets with complementary schedules reduces necessary charging stations by 45-65%.

Astronomy Research

Exosubmoon Stability Model

My research investigated the long-term stability of submoons (moons of moons) by analyzing tidal effects on orbital dynamics. I created this interactive 3D simulation to visualize how different mass ratios and orbital parameters affect submoon survival over astronomical timescales.

Research Highlights:

  • Investigated the long-term stability of sub-moons targeting survival periods exceeding 100 million years
  • Executed high-performance simulations on Google Cloud (Vertex AI) and managed extensive NASA exoplanet datasets (30,000+ records, 350+ features)
  • Analyzed key stability factors including mass ratios, orbital distances, and tidal interactions
  • Authored a formal research paper documenting findings and methodology
